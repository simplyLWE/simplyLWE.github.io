100DaysOfCode.

Not too long ago I started the #100DaysOfCode challenge.

It’s a challenge that invites people to code for one hour minimum every single day working on projects, whether that’s one big project, or several small projects. The challenge mostly provides developers motivation, community support, and accountability.

I’m not a developer. I don’t ever plan to be. But I like coding. I like reading code. I like debugging and analyzing code. I like understanding code better. And I like writing better code; similar to a mathematician who enjoys refining his proof.

And I’m doing all this in C & C++.1

And so I ventured on this challenge, not to work on mobile apps, or web apps, or any sort of big “developer-type” of project.

But instead, I am working on small projects that reinforce the fundamental computer science concepts I am learning; so that theory becomes applied understanding.

If you care to follow me along on this journey, Twitter is the place to go.2
